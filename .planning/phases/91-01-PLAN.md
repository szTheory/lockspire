<threat_model>
## Threats

- `T-91-01` Remote-JWKS fetch failures could remain flattened into generic auth failures, leaving operators unable to tell whether the URI, network path, payload, or cache posture is broken.
- `T-91-02` The repo could keep implying broader rotation support than the runtime actually ships, especially around same-`kid` replacement or other ambiguous rollover shapes.
- `T-91-03` Different remote-JWKS consumers could drift into inconsistent diagnosis rules, making client-auth and JARM support stories disagree.

## Mitigations

- Add one shared diagnosis taxonomy that converts fetcher/runtime outcomes into explicit posture categories and remediation directions before any wire-level flattening happens.
- Preserve the current bounded refresh posture and classify unsupported rollover states explicitly instead of silently treating them as ordinary signature failures.
- Reuse the same support truth across `PrivateKeyJwt`, JARM client-key resolution, and any shared remote-JWKS helper rather than introducing endpoint-specific heuristics.
</threat_model>
