<threat_model>
## Threats

- `T-89-01` Registration or operator creation could silently default `HS256`, causing the stored record to claim support that the caller never requested explicitly.
- `T-89-02` RFC 7592 replace semantics could retain stale JWT-auth signing metadata when a client switches auth method or omits the alg on update.
- `T-89-03` Auth-method and signing-alg truth could live only in `metadata`, letting discovery and admin surfaces drift from the durable record.

## Mitigations

- Require explicit `token_endpoint_auth_signing_alg=HS256` whenever `token_endpoint_auth_method=client_secret_jwt` is chosen, and reject `HS384`, `HS512`, or missing alg values.
- Clear the stored client-auth signing alg when switching away from JWT auth and reject stray `token_endpoint_auth_signing_alg` on non-JWT auth methods.
- Round-trip the typed field through `Domain.Client`, `ClientRecord`, DCR serialization, and update mapping so all downstream truth derives from one stored source.
- Enforce effective-profile denial for `client_secret_jwt` under FAPI to keep registration truth aligned with the Phase 88 runtime contract.
</threat_model>
